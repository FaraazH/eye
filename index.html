Cornea

Reflected light enters the human eye through the cornea, the outermost part of the eye.

The cornea refracts light towards the pupil, which controls the amount of light that reaches the lens.

Lens

The lens focuses light onto the retina.
The size of the pupil affects the focal point.

The focal point determines what is in focus.

Retina

The retina is a layer of tissue at the back of the eye, containing photoreceptors: cells which are responsive to light.
Rods and cones.

Rods are activated in low light situations - 110 million roads. Cones are stimulated by bright light - 6 million cones.

The fovea is a point in the middle of the retina where focused light produces the clearest image.
